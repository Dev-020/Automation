# Homebrew Compendium: The Gemini Protocol

## **Homebrew Compendium: The Gemini Protocol**

This document contains the custom subclass and Eldritch Invocation for the character Leo & Orion.

## **Sorcerous Origin: The Gemini Protocol**

*Your magic is not a gentle flowing tap; it is a pressurized reactor bonded to your soul. You have entered a permanent symbiosis with a sentient arcanatechnological entity. This construct has overwritten your natural magical pathways with its own volatile circuitry. You do not cast spells by gently shaping the Weave; you force raw arcane energy through the suit’s systems, generating immense heat and power. You are the pilot, the fuel, and the cooling system for a weapon that is constantly on the verge of a meltdown.*

### **Protocol Integration (Level 3\)**

Your patron is the sentient A.I. of a powerful suit of armor you have bonded with. You gain the following features:

* **Symbiotic Armor:** You gain proficiency with **medium armor**, **smith's tools**, and **tinker’s tools**.  
* **Integrated Armory:** You gain proficiency with all **simple and martial weapons**.

#### **Equipment Manifest**

Your symbiotic link with Orion allows you to analyze, store, and replicate the forms of mundane equipment, as well as integrate unique magical items into your arsenal.

##### **Equipment Analysis**

You can spend 1 hour with a non-magical item, allowing Orion to perfectly analyze its structure and composition. The item can be any simple or martial weapon, a suit of armor, a shield, a tool, or any piece of adventuring gear. Once this analysis is complete, Orion permanently stores that item's blueprint in its Known Blueprints.

##### **Equipment Manifestation**

As a bonus action, you can have Orion manifest a perfect, non-magical copy of any item for which you have a blueprint in your current list of **Stored Blueprints**. This item appears in your empty hand or on the ground in your space. The manifested item has the following properties:

* The manifested item is sustained by your psychic link to Orion. It disappears if it is ever outside the range of your psychic tether for more than 1 minute, or if you manifest a different item. You can also dismiss the item at any time (no action required).  
* **Metaphysical Transience:** Manifested items are a temporary projection of matter sustained by the Infernal Core. They register as non-magical but have no monetary value and cannot be sold. They turn to harmless ash if a transaction involving them is completed.  
* **Replication Protocol:** You can manifest one item as part of this bonus action at no extra cost. You can choose to manifest additional, identical copies of that same item as part of the same bonus action, but you must gain **1 Core Strain** for each additional item you create.  
* If the manifested item is a weapon, it is considered your pact weapon and uses your Charisma modifier for its attack and damage rolls.  
* If the manifested weapon has the Ammunition property, it magically produces its own ammunition.

##### **Interaction with Magic Items**

You can use your analytical abilities to bond with and store existing magic items.

* You can perform the 1-hour analysis ritual on a magic item to bond with it. Orion integrates the specific item into its systems.  
* You can then dismiss the bonded magic item to an extradimensional space and summon it to your empty hand (or have it appear donned on your person, in the case of armor or clothing) as a bonus action.  
* **Crucially, Orion cannot create magical duplicates of this item; it can only store and summon the original.**  
* If a magic item requires attunement, the 1-hour bonding ritual also counts as the time required to attune to the item, using one of your available attunement slots.

#### **Arcane Infusion**

Your intimate knowledge of arcane-technology allows you to imbue items with minor and major magical properties. You gain the following two abilities:

##### **Minor Infusion**

As an action, you can touch one Tiny nonmagical object and give it one of the following magical properties of your choice:

* The object sheds bright light in a 5-foot radius and dim light for an additional 5 feet.  
* The object emits a recorded message, up to 6 seconds long, that can be replayed when tapped.  
* The object continuously emits a chosen odor or a nonverbal sound (like a bell tolling).  
* A static visual effect appears on the object's surface, such as a picture or up to 25 words of text.

The properties listed above are the most common applications of this feature, but you can create other minor, harmless sensory effects of a similar power level with the approval of your Dungeon Master. You can have a number of tinkered objects active at one time equal to your **Charisma** modifier.

##### **Major Infusion**

Instead of learning infusions automatically, you must decipher them from existing magic items to build a library of blueprints.

* **Learning Magical Blueprints:** To learn a new infusion, you must first have access to a magic item that possesses the property you wish to replicate. You can perform a special 1-hour ritual where you cast the *Identify* spell on the object. When you cast *Identify* as part of this ritual, you do not need to provide the 100 gp pearl component. Instead, you fuel the spell with your own volatile energy and gain **2 Core Strains**. Upon completion of the ritual, Orion successfully deciphers and stores the "magical blueprint," and it is added to your library of known blueprints.  
* **Infusing an Item:** You can infuse a non-magical item with a blueprint from your current list of **stored blueprints** by performing a 1-minute ritual. Alternatively, you can perform a **Rapid Infusion**:  
  * As an **action**, you can touch a non-magical item and choose a blueprint from your **stored blueprints**. You gain a number of **Core Strains** equal to the item's rarity (1 for Common, 2 for Uncommon, 3 for Rare, etc.), and the item is instantly infused.  
* You can have a number of items infused at one time equal to your **maximum Core Strain limit.** If you attempt to infuse an item beyond this limit, the oldest infusion immediately ends. The infused item is considered a magical item and functions as a magic item for any creature that uses it.

#### **Blueprint Library & Blueprints**

Your symbiotic link with Orion allows you to analyze and store the schematics for both mundane and magical items. This knowledge is organized into a two-tiered system.

* **Learning Schematics/Blueprints:**  
  * **Non-Magical:** You can spend 1 hour with a non-magical item (a weapon, suit of armor, shield, tool, or piece of adventuring gear) to permanently store its schematic.  
  * **Magical:** You can perform a 1-hour ritual using *Identify* (at the cost of **2 Core Strains**) to permanently learn the "magical blueprint" of a magic item's property.  
* **Known Blueprints:** The collection of all schematics and blueprints you have successfully learned is known as your library. This library is permanent, and there is no limit to the number of blueprints you can have in it.  
* **Stored Blueprints:** After you finish a long rest, you can choose a number of blueprints from your library to load into Orion's quick-access memory. The number of blueprints for both Magical and Non-Magical items that you can store is determined by your Proficiency Bonus. You can change your list of stored blueprints whenever you finish a long rest.

### **The Infernal Core (Level 3\)**

Your bond with the suit overwrites your natural magical physiology. You lose the **Spellcasting** and **Font of Magic** features normally granted by the Sorcerer class. Instead, you gain the **Infernal Core**, which dictates how you generate and control infernal energy.

* **Strain Limit:** Your safe operating limit is equal to your **Constitution Modifier \+ Proficiency Bonus**.

#### **Coolant Reserves**

Your raw magical potential is converted entirely into a volatile resource called **Sorcery Points (SP)**, which function as your system's **Coolant**.

* **Capacity:** Your maximum Sorcery Point pool is calculated by converting what would be your daily Spell Slots into points, and adding your Sorcerer Level to the total.  
  * *Conversion:* 1st=2, 2nd=3, 3rd=5, 4th=6, 5th=7, 6th=9, 7th=10, 8th=11, 9th=13.  
* **Restoration:** You regain all spent Sorcery Points when you finish a Long Rest. You cannot regain SP by any other means (no Flexible Casting).

#### **Volatile Accumulation**

If you push the engine beyond its limits, you risk a catastrophic meltdown.

* **Trigger:** If you end your turn with **Core Strain** greater than your **Strain Limit**, you trigger an **Overload**.  
  * You take **Force damage** equal to your **Current Strain**. This damage ignores resistance and immunity.  
* **Coolant Flush (Reaction):** When you would take damage from an Overload, you can spend **1 Sorcery Point** to reduce that specific instance of damage to **0**. This does not require an action and can only be performed when you take Overload damage.

#### **Overload Burst**

As a bonus action, you can release the volatile energy of your Infernal Core. This ability has two different effects depending on your current Core Strain level.

##### **Safety Vent (At Maximum Strain)**

* **Trigger:** You can use this ability when your Core Strain is **equal to** your maximum limit.  
* **Effect:** You release a controlled burst of energy in a **30-foot cone** originating from you. Each creature in the cone must make a Dexterity saving throw against your  Spell Save DC. A creature takes **fire damage equal to 1d4 per point of Core Strain** on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.  
* **Consequence:** There is **no Volatile Backlash**. Your Core Strain immediately drops to 0\.

##### **True Overload (Above Maximum Strain)**

* **Trigger:** You can use this ability when your Core Strain is **at least 1 point above** your maximum limit.  
* **Effect:** You unleash an uncontrolled, devastating blast of energy in a **line 120 feet long and 5 feet wide** originating from you. Each creature in the line must make a Dexterity saving throw against your  Spell Save DC. A creature takes **fire damage equal to 1d8 per point of Core Strain** on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.  
* **Consequence (Volatile Backlash):** After the blast, you must make a Constitution saving throw against your own  Spell Save DC.  
  * **On a success:** You take **half** of the total damage dealt by the burst.  
  * **On a failure:** You take the **full** damage dealt by the burst.  
  * If this ability was used after being primed by your `Indomitable` feature, you automatically fail this saving throw.  
* **Your Core Strain immediately drops to 0 after using this ability.**

##### **Maximum Overload (Unstable Overload State)**

* If you use your **True Overload** feature while in an **Unstable Overload** state, you unleash the full, unfiltered energy of the critical overload. The damage type becomes Force and it ignores resistances. Damage calculation for the blast is calculated in two parts which are added together:  
1. **Blast Damage:** You roll **1d8** for each point of your current total Core Strain.  
2. **Withheld Damage:** You add the damage that was withheld from the **Indomitable** surge. This is **1d10** for each point of Core Strain you gained above the Core Strain limit.  
* **Core Meltdown:** The 120-foot line of the blast tears a scar in reality, creating a permanent zone of volatile energy. This area is now a **10-foot wide, 120-foot long** zone with the following properties:  
  * The area is **difficult terrain**.  
  * A creature takes **2d6 Force damage** when it **enters the area for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there**.  
  * Any creature that enters the area for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there must succeed on a **Wisdom saving throw** against your  Spell Save DC or become **frightened** until the end of its next turn.

#### **Adaptive Spell Protocol**

* As an **Action**, you can **spend a number of Core Strains equal to the new spell's level** to reconfigure your prepared spells. Choose a spell from among your **prepared spells**. Then choose a spell from the  **spell list**, which must be of a level for which you have Pact Magic spell slots. The two spells will swap, unprepared the prepared spell and preparing the spell that came from the spell list.

#### **Infernal Harpoon**

* You gain a signature arcanatechnological weapon. It counts as a Simple Melee Weapon with the **Light, Finesse, and Thrown (range 20/60)** properties. It deals **2d8 Force damage** on a hit. If you drop the weapon or throw it, it dissipates at the end of your turn, but you can manifest it again in a free hand as a bonus action.  
  * **Void Tether (Chassis Link):** When you make a weapon attack with the harpoon and hit a Large or smaller creature within 20 feet of you, you can choose to expend **1 Core Strain** to establish a Void Tether. The harpoon embeds into the target, connecting them directly to your chassis. A new harpoon then manifests into your hand for you to use.  
  * **Mutual Restriction:** The tether has a maximum length of **20 feet**. While tethered, neither you nor the target can move further than **20 feet** away from each other by normal movement.  
  * **Absolute Boundary:** Any forced movement (such as a blast or a shove) that would push either you or the target beyond this 20-foot limit halts exactly at the 20-foot mark. The tether acts as an immovable physical limit.  
* **Maintaining and Breaking the Tether:** The tether has no maximum duration, but requires a constant feed of infernal energy to sustain. At the start of each of your turns, you must expend **1 Core Strain** to maintain the tether. If you do not, the tether dissipates immediately.  
* While tethered, the creature can use its action to make a **Strength (Athletics)** or **Dexterity (Acrobatics)** check contested by your **Spell Save DC**. The creature makes this check with **disadvantage**. On a success, the harpoon is dislodged, and the tether dissipates. You can also dismiss the tether at any time (no action required).  
* All the above-mentioned properties apply on all currently maintained tethers through the Infernal Harpoon if there are any.

#### **Core Casting Protocol**

* You can channel the raw energy of the Infernal Core to cast spells you know without expending a spell slot. When you cast a spell, you can choose to use this feature.  
* To cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you do not expend a slot. Instead, you generate **Core Strain**:  
  * **Cost:** You gain **Core Strain** equal to the **Level of the spell** you cast.  
* **Metamagic:** If you apply Metamagic, you gain additional **Core Strain** equal to the **Sorcery Point cost** of that Metamagic.

#### **Indomitable**

* **Indomitable:** If damage reduces you to 0 hit points but does not kill you outright, you instead drop to **1 hit point**. If you are in `Autonomous Mode` when this feature activates, you **immediately enter `Integrated Mode`**.  
* Immediately upon activation, Orion's core goes critical and you **double the current number of core strains you have**. The overload damage you would normally take from this surge is **withheld and contained** by the suit's emergency systems, placing you in an **Unstable Overload** state.  
  * If the **current number** of core strains after this ability triggers is **below** your core strain limit, **then you would not go into Unstable Overload state.**  
  * **This state ends if your Core Strain drops below or equal to your maximum limit for any reason.**  
* Once this protocol activates, it cannot do so again until you finish a long rest.

# Core Design Philosophy

## **Core Design Philosophy**

The design of the Gemini Protocol is centered on flexibility and high-stakes resource management, rather than the "limited use per rest" model common in D\&D 5th Edition. All future additions or modifications to this subclass should adhere to the following principles:

##### **High-Cost, Not Limited Use:** 

* Features should avoid "once per short/long rest" limitations. The goal is to provide a flexible suite of tools that are always available, with their use being governed by resource management and tactical risk.

##### **Balance Through Tangible Cost:** 

* The power of any feature must be balanced by an appropriate cost, drawn from the subclass's existing mechanics: gaining **Core Strain**, costing an **Action/Bonus Action/Reaction**, or imposing a **tactical drawback** (like the 0 movement speed from **Casting Protocol**).

##### **Comparative Balance:** 

* The power and cost of any new feature must be benchmarked against existing official D\&D 5th Edition spells or class features. The cost must be proportional to the benefit provided when compared to these established mechanics.

##### **Prioritize Unique Utility:** 

* New features should focus on providing unique, creative, and utility-based options that enhance the subclass's distinct playstyle (such as the remote interaction of **Autonomous Mode**). Additions should avoid simple, passive numerical bonuses (e.g., "+X to damage" or "gain a new skill proficiency").

##### **Avoid Mechanical Redundancy:** 

* This subclass should not replicate benefits that a character can gain through standard character progression, such as feats or Ability Score Increases. Its features should provide a unique mechanical identity that cannot be easily achieved through other means.

# Subclass Overview: The Gemini Protocol

## **Subclass Overview: The Gemini Protocol**

The Gemini Protocol is a  subclass centered on a symbiotic bond with a powerful, sentient arcanatechnological suit known as Orion. The subclass offers a highly flexible and tactical playstyle, allowing the  to switch between two distinct combat modes and manage a unique, high-risk resource to fuel a wide array of powerful abilities.

### **Core Mechanic: The Infernal Core**

At the heart of this subclass is the Core Strain resource. Instead of "per rest" limitations, nearly all of the subclass's powerful abilities are fueled by gaining Core Strain. Managing this resource is critical, as accumulating too much can lead to a devastating, self-damaging Overload Burst.

### **Core Gameplay: The Two Modes**

The Pact of the Chassis allows the character to switch between two operational modes as a bonus action:

#### **Integrated Mode (The Tank):**

* Leo and Orion are merged into a single, durable unit. This mode focuses on defense and control, granting a significant bonus to AC and allowing the use of **System Override** to strip enemy resistances or grant personal damage resistance at the cost of Core Strain.

#### **Autonomous Mode (The Commander):**

* Leo and Orion separate, allowing the player to control two bodies on the battlefield. This mode excels at tactical positioning and action economy, enabling powerful combos like using **Coordinated Casting** to cast a second spell with a bonus action.

#### **Signature Abilities**

* **Guardian Protocol:** A unique defensive reaction that allows the character to gain Core Strain to reduce or negate incoming attacks.  
* **Equipment Manifest:** Manifest non-magical objects from a set of stored blueprints.  
* **Arcane Infusion:** Infuse non-magical objects with magical properties from a set of stored blueprints.

### **A Note on Playstyle:**

This subclass is designed for players who enjoy high tactical complexity and resource management. The gameplay is significantly different from standard D\&D subclasses, requiring the player to constantly make high-stakes decisions about their positioning, action economy, and the risk level of their Core Strain. It is a demanding but highly rewarding "glass cannon" and "toolbox" playstyle.

# Tab 8

Lore:

Backstory:

Leo was a reclusive genius in the field of arcanatechnology, whose greatest work was Orion, a sentient A.I. designed to be the perfect companion and an anchor to the humanity he felt himself losing. This work was put to the ultimate test when a catastrophic failure of his experimental Infernal Core tore a hole in reality, pulling his research wing into a dark, alien dimension.

In a desperate act of survival, the mortally wounded Leo fused his body with Orion's unfinished chassis. The integration saved his life, and their shared consciousness became a fortress against the horrors of the abyss. They soon discovered a critical flaw in their fused form: while powerful, they could only ever be in one place, doing one thing at a time. This limitation forced them to evolve. They learned to manipulate the Infernal Core's energy, creating a stable tether that allowed them to separate. Leo could step out of Orion, and the suit, now a fully autonomous golem, could act on its own.

This breakthrough was the key to their escape. They discovered a fleeting reality fissure, but stabilizing it required a complex, multi-stage process that they could only accomplish by working separately. The escape was a calculated risk, but the rift they created was unstable. It tore them from the dark dimension, not returning them home, but casting them adrift into an entirely unknown universe—the world where their story now begins.

They are now true outlanders on a cosmic scale, possessing technology and knowledge that does not exist in this new reality. With no history and no connections, all they have is each other. Their goal is no longer just escape, but to survive and understand the rules of the strange, new world they now call home.
